Acta · design system · component sheet

The artifact as an engineering record

Paper first, screen second. Provenance opens every document; evidence is cited into a register; decisions are numbered, stamped, and waiting for a signature. Nothing competes with the text.

Draft — awaiting review Validates 4 artifact families

Summary

Acta is the design system for HTML artifacts produced by the agent-skills collection. One recognizable shell (provenance → title → summary → sections → export → colophon) wraps a free instrument per job. This sheet shows the foundations, the 22 component contracts, and four realistic family proofs: Document, Compare, Explore, and Edit. Two borrowed patterns — decision gates and the waypoint map — are restated in Acta’s idiom.

Space, geometry, rules, motion, breakpoints

Foundation scales
Space4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 px (--acta-s1…s8). Sections breathe at s5–s6; the page ends at s8.
Radius0 by default; 2 px on controls only. No pills, no rounded cards.
Rules1 px hairline (--acta-rule) divides; 2 px accent bar cites; 3 px double rule (--acta-rule-strong) opens and closes the record. Boxes are earned, not default.
ShadowNone. Hierarchy comes from type, space, rules, and tint.
Motion--acta-quick (90 ms linear), color/border only. Nothing moves; nothing essential depends on it. prefers-reduced-motion strips the rest.
BreakpointsTwo: 960 px (rails collapse) and 720 px (single column). Layout must reflow to 320 px with horizontal scrolling only inside code, tables, and diagrams.
Inverse surfaceScarce emphasis: code and at most one TL;DR-critical block per artifact. If everything is emphasized, nothing is.

§4Decisions & comparison

DecisionCard (register row)numbered, stateful, reply-to-change
D-01

Idempotency key: header X-Meridian-Delivery

UUID per event, generated on enqueue. Consumers drop repeated keys, not repeated bodies.

Status: proposed · Reversible: yes · Change if: consumers cannot read headers.

Alternative considered: SHA-256 of the payload

Avoids the header but collides with legitimately identical events (two equal charges, same day). Rejected for semantic ambiguity.

One-line reply to change it: “Use a payload hash instead of the header.”

Question 1 · failure behavior

The dispatcher dies after receiving a 200 but before closing the attempt. On restart?

Correct. The open attempt forces a re-send; idempotency (D-01) makes it a consumer no-op.

Not quite. Re-read evidence E1 and decision D-01: the re-send happens, the repeated key neutralizes it.

Question 2 · rollback

Slice 1 must be rolled back after a day in production. What is required?

Correct. Additive migration + flag = reversible without data surgery.

Not quite. See the gate checklist: additive migration behind a flag rolls back with flag-off + down migration.

Rule
